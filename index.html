Prasiddha Shakya (Sid)

I am a fourth-year PhD student in Economics at Colorado State University (CSU) currently researching on topics pertaining to economics of health and education. My dissertation basically focuses on two different areas of interest of mine. The first area of research looks at the impacts of TRIO-funded programs on college outcomes for first-generation students here in the US. My second area of work looks at the possibility of "social remittances" from migration. Utilizing the DHS surveys from Nepal and India, I evaluate whether having someone you know abroad changes behaviors of households back home.

I received my undergraduate degree in Mathematics from RCNJ where I also minored in Computer Science and Economics. I think this has led me to become a huge proponent of interdisciplinary research and my research aims to utilize tools from various disciplines outside of those used in the "traditional" Economics literature. I also do a lot of quantitative work of the Institute for Reseach in Social Science (IRISS) at CSU which has further strengthened my believe that we need to incorporate multi-disciplinary approach to address questions in developmental economics and economics of health and educations (areas I am primarily interested in).

I am currently actively trying to find datasets that look at whether family structure/specific work professions have significant impact on both pre and post partum depression rates in developed countries.
